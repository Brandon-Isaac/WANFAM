Project Overview

Main Objective

To develop a comprehensive, affordable, and user-friendly web-based livestock management system specifically tailored for small to medium-scale farmers in Kenya.

  • Target Users: Small to medium-scale farmers (5-50 animals)
  • Project Type: Web Application Development
  • Duration: 11 Weeks
  • Platform: Progressive Web Application
3/12

The Problem

Cost Barriers

Existing systems require expensive hardware that small-scale farmers cannot afford

Complexity Issues

Current solutions have steep learning curves and assume high technical literacy

Limited Offline Support

Most systems fail when internet connectivity is poor or intermittent

Language Barriers

Most solutions are only available in English, limiting adoption

Result: 78% of small-scale farmers still rely on paper-based systems

4/12

Market Gap Analysis

Current Solutions vs. Market Needs

iCow

Strengths: SMS reminders, low-cost

Gap: Limited record-keeping, no productivity tracking

Cowlar

Strengths: Comprehensive tracking

Gap: Hardware-dependent, expensive

Herdwatch

Strengths: User-friendly

Gap: Not adapted for Kenyan conditions

Market Opportunity: 2.5M small-scale farmers with no comprehensive, affordable solution

5/12

Wanfam SLHTS Solution

Integrated Health Management

Vaccination tracking, treatment records, and health observations in one interface

Smart Feeding Scheduler

Automated reminders with cost tracking and ROI calculation

Productivity Analysis

Track milk production, breeding success, and weight gain against costs

Accessibility First

Bilingual support (English/Swahili) with offline functionality
